## Visual Matching Criteria (Quick Reference)

### MUST MATCH
- Trail surface: Bare dirt (brown/tan)
- Trail width: 3-8 feet
- Forest type: Deciduous (light green canopy)

### SHOULD MATCH
- Trail orientation: N-S or NW-SE
- Trail pattern: Linear with gentle curves
- Canopy density: 50-80% coverage

### NICE TO HAVE
- Parking/trailhead visible
- Cell towers nearby
- Trail intersections identifiable

## Notes & Observations

### Challenges
1. **Resolution Limits:** Google Earth ~1m resolution may be marginal for 3-6 foot trails
2. **Canopy Coverage:** Deciduous forest with 50-80% coverage may obscure trails
3. **Image Dating:** Satellite images may be several years old
4. **Seasonal Variation:** Best visibility in fall/spring, worst in summer

### Success Factors
1. **Trail Width:** 5-8 foot trails should be visible as 1-2 pixels
2. **Color Contrast:** Brown dirt vs. green vegetation = good detection
3. **Linear Features:** Railroad grades and old roads = high visibility
4. **Open Canopy:** Areas with canopy gaps = better trail visibility
